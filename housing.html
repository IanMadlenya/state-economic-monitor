Housing

National house prices increased 4.5 percent from the third quarter of 2013 to the third quarter of 2014 (figure TK). Nevada (10.3 percent) had the greatest increase in house prices during the period. Three other states (California, Hawaii, and Nevada) also had prices increase by more than 8 percent. Connecticut (-1.3 percent) was the only state where house prices fell over the year. Twelve states had price increase less than 2 percent and four (Delaware, Maryland, Vermont, and Virginia) had less than 1 percent growth during this period. National house prices in the third quarter of 2014 were still 6.2 percent below the peak in house prices during the first quarter of 2007 (figure TK).

House prices in 34 states are lower than they were during the first quarter of 2007. While Nevada had the greatest year-over-year growth of any state in the third quarter, it also is the state furthest from its 2007 prices (-35 percent). Arizona, California, and Florida also are more than 20 percent below their 2007 prices despite enjoying year-over-year price growth of 6 percent or more. North Dakota (43 percent) has had the most price growth since the first quarter of 2007. Four other states (Colorado, Oklahoma, South Dakota, and Texas) and DC have also had 10 percent or more growth in house prices since the first quarter of 2007.
