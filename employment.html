Employment

Breaking down state data on unemployment rates, total (nonfarm) payroll employment, and government employment.
Roll over the bar charts, scatter plots, maps, and legends to see additional data.

The national unemployment rate was 5.4 percent in April 2015. The unemployment rate in the District of Columbia (7.5 percent) was the highest in the nation. Among the states, the highest rates were in Nevada (7.1 percent), West Virginia (7.0 percent), Alaska (6.7 percent), South Carolina (6.7 percent), and Louisiana and Mississippi (both 6.6 percent). The lowest April unemployment rate was in Nebraska (2.5 percent). Eight other states had rates below 4 percent: North Dakota (3.1 percent), Utah (3.4 percent), South Dakota (3.6 percent), Vermont (3.6 percent), Minnesota (3.7 percent), Idaho (3.8 percent), Iowa (3.8 percent), and New Hampshire (3.8 percent).

The national unemployment rate fell 0.8 percentage points between April 2014 and April 2015. Five states saw the unemployment rate increase over the year: Louisiana (0.9 points), South Carolina (0.6 points), North Dakota (0.4 points), South Dakota (0.2 points), and West Virginia (0.2 points). Alaska, Kansas, New Jersey, and Wyoming saw unemployment drop but only 0.2 points. Among these poor-performing states, five also had April unemployment rates of 6.5 percent or greater: Alaska, Louisiana, New Jersey, South Carolina, and West Virginia. The largest unemployment rate declines over the year were in Michigan (-2.1 points), Kentucky (-2.0 points), Rhode Island (-2.0 points), Oregon (-1.8 points), and California (-1.5 points). The unemployment rate dropped more than 1 point in another nine states. Among these better-performing states, seven had April unemployment rates below 5 percent: Colorado, Delaware, Kentucky, Idaho, Maine, Massachusetts, and Wisconsin.

Total national (public and private) nonfarm payroll employment increased 2.2 percent from April 2014 to April 2015. Total employment over the 12-month period only decreased in West Virginia (-0.7 percent). The states with the smallest increases in employment were Louisiana, Missouri, and Montana (all 0.7 percent). Another seven states had increases of 0.8 percent or 0.9 percent. The largest increase in employment was in Utah (4.0 percent). Employment grew more than 3 percent over the past 12 months in six states: Florida (3.6 percent), Washington (3.6 percent), Nevada (3.4 percent), Oregon (3.4 percent), Idaho (3.1 percent), and Georgia (3.0 percent).

Total public-sector employment increased 0.3 percent from April 2014 to April 2015. Public-sector employment declined in 11 states and was unchanged in Hawaii and Utah. The largest drops were in Louisiana (-1.5 percent), Montana (-1.3 percent), New Hampshire (-1.3 percent), and Pennsylvania (-1.1 percent). Louisiana, Montana, and New Hampshire had total employment increase less than 1 percent and Pennsylvania’s increase was 1.1 percent. Public-sector employment increased 2 percent or more in Washington (2.2 percent), Oregon (2.1 percent), and Idaho (2.0 percent). Total employment increased more than 3 percent over the past year in all three states.
