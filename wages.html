Wages

National real weekly earnings (i.e., earnings adjusted for inflation) for all US private employees averaged $864 in November 2014 (figure TK). In the states, average weekly earnings ranged from $681 in Arkansas to $1,023 in Massachusetts. Arkansas was the only state with average earnings less than $700 in November 2014. Twenty-six states had real average earnings less than $800. In addition to Massachusetts, Washington ($1,009) was the only other state with average weekly earnings above $1,000. The District of Columbia’s average weekly earnings were $1,463 in November 2014. Compared with November 2013, national real average weekly earnings (adjusted for inflation) increased by 2.8 percent (figure TK).

Texas had the greatest increase in real wages (5.9 percent), followed by Georgia (5.6 percent) and Kansas (5.5 percent). Six other states (Connecticut, Hawaii, Indian, Massachusetts, Utah, and Washington) also had real average weekly wage growth greater than 4 percent over the past year. In total, 41 states and DC had positive real wage growth over the year. New Mexico (-2.4 percent) had the greatest decline in real average wages between November 2013 and November 2014. Four other states (Arkansas, Arizona, New Hampshire, and Rhode Island) also had real average wages fall by more than 1 percent over the period.
