Employment

North Dakota’s 2.7 percent unemployment rate was the lowest among the states. Idaho, Minnesota, Nebraska, South Dakota, and Utah were the five other states with unemployment rates below 4 percent in November. In total, 29 states had unemployment rates below 6 percent in November (up from 25 in August). Mississippi’s 7.3 percent unemployment rate was the highest in November. California, Georgia, Oregon, and Rhode Island were the four other states with unemployment rates above 7 percent. The District of Columbia’s (DC) unemployment rate was 7.4 percent in November.

Among the states, Illinois (-2.8 points) experienced the largest drop in unemployment. In total, the unemployment rate fell in 43 states and DC during that period. The unemployment rate fell by more than 1 percent in 22 states. The unemployment rate increased in four states (Alaska, Louisiana, Iowa, and West Virginia) over the past year. Louisiana (0.9 points) had the largest increase over the past year. The unemployment rate was unchanged between November 2013 and November 2014 in North Dakota, Vermont, and Wyoming.

North Dakota’s 4.9 percent increase in total employment was the largest among the states. Texas (3.9 percent) and Utah (3.3 percent) were the other two states with total employment increases greater than 3 percent. Alaska (-0.3 percent) and Mississippi (-0.1 percent) were the only states where total employment declined over the past year. The states with the next lowest total employment gains were New Jersey and Virginia—both had 0.4 percent increases in total employment.

Furthermore, while total employment declined in only two states over the year, public-sector employment declined in 19 states. Public-sector employment fell by more than 1 percent in two states: Alaska (-1.8 percent) and Louisiana (-1.8 percent). Utah (2.8 percent) had the largest increase in public-sector employment from November 2013 to November 2014. Iowa and Oregon also had public-sector employment increase by 2 percent or more.
